## The 7 sentence patterns

<div className="mb-3 flex items-center gap-2">
  <Badge variant="main-quest">PATTERNS</Badge>
</div>

1. **Participle tails.** Sentences ending with "-ing" phrases that add fake analysis. "...highlighting the importance of...", "...ensuring continued growth...", "...reflecting broader trends...". Cut every one.
2. **Significance inflation.** "Stands as", "serves as", "marks a", "represents a shift", "setting the stage for". Say what it IS, not what it REPRESENTS.
3. **Negative parallelisms.** "Not just X, but Y." "It's not about X, it's about Y." "It's a Y, not an X." Pick one side, or split into two sentences.
4. **Rule of three.** "Adjective, adjective, and adjective." "Short phrase, short phrase, and short phrase." One or two is fine. Three in a row reads as AI.
5. **False ranges.** "From X to Y" where X and Y are not on a real scale. "From strategy to execution" is fine. "From brand building to operational excellence" is meaningless AI filler.
6. **Copula avoidance.** AI replaces "is" with "serves as", "stands as", "marks", "represents". If "is" or "has" works, use it.
7. **Elegant variation.** AI avoids repeating words by cycling synonyms (company to organization to entity to venture). In a 60-word piece, just use the company name.

## The 4 gut checks

<div className="mb-3 flex items-center gap-2">
  <Badge variant="xp">GUT CHECKS</Badge>
  <span className="font-mono text-xs uppercase tracking-wider text-fd-muted-foreground">run after every draft</span>
</div>

1. **The "anyone" test.** Could this sentence describe any company? If yes, delete it. AI generalizes, humans specify.
2. **The "so what" test.** Does any sentence say something is important without saying WHY? That's AI puffery. Cut it.
3. **The "would I text this" test.** Read the sentence out loud as if texting a smart friend. Any sentence that sounds weird means rewrite.
4. **The opener/closer test.** Read the opening line and the closing line. Could either appear in any landing page? If yes, rewrite both. Generic openers and closers are the clearest AI tell.

## Tone

<div className="mb-3 flex items-center gap-2">
  <Badge variant="xp">HUMAN TELLS</Badge>
</div>

AI defaults to formal, uncontracted, frictionless prose. Human writing has rough edges.

- **Contractions.** Use I've, I'm, don't, won't, can't. AI writes "I have", "I am", "do not". Uncontracted reads as an instant tell.
- **Sentence starters.** Occasionally start with "And" or "But". AI almost never does this.
- **Imperfection moment.** Every landing page or email benefits from one: a challenge faced, a limitation acknowledged, something you learned the hard way. Perfect reads as AI.
- **Paragraph rhythm.** Vary lengths. Do not open every paragraph the same way. AI defaults to parallel structure because it is statistically safe.

## Structural tells to cut

<div className="mb-3 flex items-center gap-2">
  <Badge variant="boss">STRUCTURE</Badge>
</div>

- **Bold-colon lists.** "Point: explanation" format. Never in body copy.
- **Emoji decorations.** Not in landing pages.
- **Em dashes.** Use periods or commas. Zero tolerance.
- **Curly quotes.** Use straight quotes and apostrophes only.
- **Section summaries.** "In summary", "Overall", "In conclusion". Never.
