## Principle 1. One brain, many executors

<div className="mb-3 flex items-center gap-2">
  <Badge variant="main-quest">ROUTING</Badge>
</div>

Your Claude Project is the brain. Every other tool is an executor. The brain holds the plan and the context. Executors run single jobs.

- **Copy drafting** goes to ChatGPT (or Gemini when ChatGPT degrades).
- **Fact-check and niche research** goes to Perplexity.
- **Hero image and logo** goes to Gemini Nano Banana or Ideogram.
- **Long-doc summarization** goes to Gemini 2.5 Flash (1M context).
- **Builds** go to Framer or Bolt.

The brain drafts a tight brief. The executor does the heavy-lift work. The brain does a red-team read when the output comes back. This pattern stretches your Claude free-tier messages 5x further than if you use Claude as the drafter.

## Principle 2. Shorter prompts beat longer ones

<div className="mb-3 flex items-center gap-2">
  <Badge variant="main-quest">TOKEN EFFICIENCY</Badge>
</div>

2026 research consensus across Anthropic, Lakera, Thomas Wiegold, Microsoft, IBM, and Google Cloud converges on four points:

1. **Attention scales quadratically with length.** Long prompts do not make models smarter. They dilute focus.
2. **Examples beat instructions.** One filled-in sample answer beats two paragraphs of "make sure to".
3. **Structure beats length.** Define the output contract (what you want back), not the process (how to think).
4. **Most failures come from ambiguity.** Not from model limits.

Every prompt in [the Spellbook](/docs/prompts) follows this shape: one-line intent, output contract, voice rules, one worked example. That is why they are short and run well on every free-tier model.

## Principle 4. Your Claude Project is a capacity multiplier

<div className="mb-3 flex items-center gap-2">
  <Badge variant="main-quest">CONTEXT</Badge>
</div>

Files inside the Project knowledge persist across every chat you open inside that Project. Upload them once on Day 1 morning (see [Set up your Claude Project](/docs/claude-project/setup)), and you get four upsides:

1. Context is loaded for free. No re-pasting the tool matrix or the 4-box on every turn.
2. Claude's quality is higher because it has the ground rules.
3. You burn fewer messages because prompts can be shorter.
4. Multiple chats across the weekend stay consistent. Day 2 Claude remembers what Day 1 Claude decided.

## Compressor move

<div className="mb-3 flex items-center gap-2">
  <Badge variant="xp">PATTERN</Badge>
  <span className="font-mono text-xs uppercase tracking-wider text-fd-muted-foreground">the Gemini Flash sandwich</span>
</div>

When you have raw research that is too big to paste into Claude (Reddit threads, competitor pages, long transcripts):

1. Paste the whole mess into Gemini 2.5 Flash. Say "summarize into a 500-word briefing. Preserve all numbers, quotes, and named sources. No filler."
2. Copy the 500-word briefing.
3. Paste the briefing into Claude. Now ask Claude to synthesize.

You spent 0 Claude messages on compression. You saved the Claude quota for the reasoning step where it actually matters.
