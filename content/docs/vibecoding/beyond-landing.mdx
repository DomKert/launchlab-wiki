The default weekend ends with a live landing page and a waitlist. That's the right discipline for 72 hours. But some shapes (preorders, single-product e-com, SaaS-lite) genuinely need more than a form. Below is the minimum tool to plug in for each shape, not a full tutorial. Most students should not try to build the full product. Capture intent now, build the rest after.

## Preorder or one-off checkout

[Stripe Payment Link](https://stripe.com/payments/payment-links). 5 min to create, paste the link as your CTA on the landing page. Money goes to Stripe, you keep the page in Bolt or Framer.

Use this when you have one thing to sell, you want to take payment now, and you don't need a full cart.

## Single-product e-com (live now)

Same as preorder, but the product ships immediately rather than later. Stripe Payment Link still works. [Lemon Squeezy](https://lemonsqueezy.com) is the alternative for digital products (courses, downloads, software) since it handles tax compliance globally.

Use this when you have one product live and shippable. Don't try to build a multi-product store this weekend.

## Waitlist with later invite

Tally form embed plus Mailchimp or Resend for the eventual broadcast. Both already in the [Build stack](/docs/tools/build). Captures email, lets you broadcast when invites go out.

Use this when the product isn't live yet but you want a list to notify when it is. Default for invite-only brands like Back Room.

## SaaS-lite (signup + dashboard)

[Supabase](https://supabase.com) for auth and database. Plug it into Bolt's scaffold. Free tier handles 50K monthly users plus 500MB DB.

Use this when you're genuinely building a tool people log into. Real but ambitious. If you don't already know what an auth flow looks like, default to landing plus waitlist instead.

## The framing

Three days isn't enough to build a real product. It's enough to build the storefront and capture buyers. The page is the storefront. The Stripe Payment Link is the till. Everything else is a Day 4+ problem.
