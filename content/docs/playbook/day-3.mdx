## The Raid Hour

50 students publish their launch post inside one 60-minute window. Each student brings 8 to 10 genuine engagers from their own warm network, ready to like, comment, share, and convert.

The mechanic is squad-plus-friends, not 50-way cross-engagement. Platforms in 2026 detect repeated cross-engagement between the same cluster of accounts (LinkedIn pod detection hit 97% accuracy this year, with documented 96% reach drops). What they do not detect is 50 distinct accounts each drawing on their own real networks in the same window. The cohort still launches together. The conversion energy comes from your circle of 20.

Wave structure is a soft schedule, not compliance. Squads 1 to 5 publish in the first 30 minutes, 6 to 10 in the second 30. Post when your warm network is most likely online.

Reddit excluded from the Raid Hour. Reddit's vote-manipulation policy bans coordinated voting from external groups, with detection across IP, timing, and device fingerprints. If your ICP lives on Reddit, post 24 to 48 hours after the Raid Hour from your own karma-built account, following the 90/10 rule.

Soft platform quotas. LinkedIn 100 to 150 likes per day. X under 5 likes per hour as a conservative ceiling. IG 20 likes per hour for new accounts, 30 to 50 for accounts older than 6 months. Guardrails, not targets.
