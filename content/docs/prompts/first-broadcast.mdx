## Why this works

Most first-time builders delay this for days because it feels scary. One prompt gets your first SMS and first email drafted in 10 minutes. Ship it rough. Your list would rather hear from you than wait for polished.

## Prompt

```txt
I'm [PRODUCT], launching [WHAT + WHEN]. Write me the first SMS and the first email to my waitlist.

SMS. Under 160 characters, plain English, no links without context. Feels like a text from a person they know. 3 variants.

Email.
- Subject (7 words max)
- Body (3 short paragraphs)
- Single CTA
- Sign-off that matches how I actually talk

Give me 3 subject line variants, one body, one CTA. After, tell me which subject + SMS variant pair to ship.

Voice rule. If it sounds like MailChimp, delete it. I'd rather send something a little raw than something polished enough to be ignored.
```

## Back Room sample answer (abbreviated)

> **SMS variants.**
> 1. *The Back Room. First drop opens Sat 9pm CET. 42 pieces. One hour head start for the list.*
> 2. *Hey, it's Domen from The Back Room. Saturday 9pm, first drop. Tap for the preview.*
> 3. *Sat 9pm CET. 42 European vintage pieces, the list sees them first.*
>
> **Email subject options.** 1. *Saturday 9pm, the first drop.* 2. *42 pieces, you first.* 3. *The Back Room opens Saturday.*
>
> **Body (3 paragraphs).** *This is the one you signed up for. Saturday at 9pm CET, I'm opening the first drop of The Back Room. 42 pieces, sourced over the last six weeks around Italy and France.*
>
> *You'll get the preview link one hour before it goes to the general list. If you see something, grab it. Sold is sold. I'm not restocking.*
>
> *See you Saturday. Any questions, just reply.*
>
> **CTA.** *(link to the preview page)*
>
> **Ship pair.** SMS 1 + Subject 1. Short, plain, gives the when first.
