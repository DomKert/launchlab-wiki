## Why this works

AI-written copy has tells. Readers clock them in 3 seconds and bounce. Run every block of text through this at the end of Day 2 and again on Day 3 before launch. It catches what you stop seeing after the fifth revision.

## Prompt

```txt
Rewrite this copy so it passes as human-written. Paste the copy inside the <copy> tags below.

Step 1. List the top 10 most overused AI filler verbs (the ones that scream "a bot wrote this").

Step 2. Apply these rules to the copy.
- Cut every filler verb from your Step 1 list
- Cut em dashes, bold-colon lists, and rule-of-three adjective stacks
- Cut "not just X, but Y" parallelisms
- Use contractions wherever they fit
- Every sentence should pass "would a 19-year-old send this in a text"
- If a sentence doesn't add information, delete it
- No opening with "In today's fast-paced world" or any corporate throat-clearing

Output.
- The rewritten copy
- A 3-bullet list of the biggest changes you made and why

<copy>
[PASTE YOUR ORIGINAL AI-WRITTEN COPY HERE]
</copy>
```

## Back Room sample answer (abbreviated)

> **The model's top-10 filler-verb list.** Varies between runs, but you'll recognize them the second you see them. They're the same 8 to 12 corporate verbs you already know.
>
> **Before.** A 3-sentence About paragraph built on five filler verbs, a "discerning shoppers" identity frame, a "community of enthusiasts" closer, and an "innovative platform" phrase in the middle. 45 words, no concrete facts, zero specifics about the product.
>
> **After.**
> *The Back Room is an invite-only list for European vintage drops. One release a month, 40 to 60 pieces, all picked by hand. No algorithm, no flood of listings, no hunting. You're either on the list or you're not.*
>
> **Biggest changes.**
> - Replaced every vague identity phrase with what the thing actually is and does.
> - Cut all five filler verbs in a single pass.
> - Deleted the "community" closer because it added nothing and sounded like a pitch deck.
