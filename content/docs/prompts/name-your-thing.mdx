## Why this works

Naming is where first-time builders stall for 2 hours and lose the morning. This gives you 15 candidates in one run, ranked, with .com guesses. Pick fast, move on. You can always rename after launch.

## Prompt

```txt
I'm naming my [THING, e.g., invite-only vintage drop club]. Give me 15 name candidates across three categories:

- 5 short and punchy (1 to 2 syllables each)
- 5 evocative or metaphorical (a word, object, or place that hints at the vibe)
- 5 direct and descriptive (says what it does, no magic)

For each name, note:
- Does the .com likely exist (your best guess, flag as "check it")
- One-line rationale (why it works or doesn't)

Rules: no fake startup-fundraising names (no "-ly," "-ify," "-io" fluff unless it genuinely rhymes). No names that need pronunciation explanation. Must be sayable out loud without feeling embarrassed.

End with your top 3 ranked S / A / B, plus which .com alternative (.so, .xyz, .co) you'd pick if the .com is gone.
```

## Back Room sample answer (abbreviated)

> **Short and punchy.** Vault (likely taken), Drop (very likely taken), Archive (check it), Cellar (.com check it), Held (probably open).
>
> **Evocative.** The Back Room (the actual pick), The Curator's List (long but strong), Atelier 12, Private View, After Hours.
>
> **Direct.** Invite Vintage, Monthly Vintage, Curated Drops, Private Vintage, The Drop List.
>
> **Top 3.** S *The Back Room* (already the brand, evokes private access, memorable). A *Private View* (gallery reference fits the audience). B *Archive* (good but over-indexed in fashion resale).
>
> **.com fallback.** For *The Back Room*, try `thebackroomvintage.com` or `.co` if the bare name is gone.
