## Why this works

Demo Day is 10 squads by 6 minutes each, peer voting across 4 categories (Most Traction, Most Creative Business, Best Pivot, Best Distribution Hustle). You need a 30-second pitch you can say on camera without reading. This gives you three variants tuned to different voting categories, so you pick the one that matches what actually happened to you this weekend.

## Prompt

```txt
I built [PRODUCT] in 3 days at TETR LaunchLab. My customer is [ONE LINE]. My launch happened [WHAT HAPPENED: signups, sales, feedback, crickets, pivots, anything real, be honest].

Write me a 30-second Demo Day pitch (roughly 75 words, 150 max) I can deliver on camera in a 6-minute squad slot. Voting categories are Most Traction, Most Creative Business, Best Pivot, Best Distribution Hustle. Aim for whichever of those I actually have a shot at.

Output 3 variants:
1. The "traction" version (numbers, sales, signups, engagement)
2. The "story" version (why I picked this, what broke, what I learned)
3. The "distribution hustle" version (where I posted, what worked, what died)

Each variant has a clear open, one concrete specific in the middle, and an ask at the end (vote, sign up, DM me).

Voice rule: these are going to be watched by 49 other first-time builders and their families. No corporate. No "excited to share." Lead with the thing you're most proud of that actually happened.

Tell me which of the 3 is strongest given what I told you, and which voting category it fits.
```

## Back Room sample answer (abbreviated)

> Assume 18 waitlist signups, 0 sales yet, 2 pieces pre-claimed via DM.
>
> **Traction variant.** *I'm Domen, I built The Back Room. 18 people on the waitlist in 72 hours, 2 pieces already pre-claimed before the first drop opens. Invite-only European vintage, 40 to 60 pieces a month. Saturday at 9pm CET, drop one goes live. If curated vintage beats marketplace chaos for you, the invite link is in the chat. Vote me if the number of strangers saying yes surprised you.*
>
> **Story variant.** *Everyone told me invite-only was going to flop in 3 days. I almost pivoted to a regular Shopify store Friday night. Instead I built the waitlist flow Saturday morning and 18 people asked for invites without ever seeing the product. I learned that scarcity is trust, not a gimmick. That changed how I'm pricing the next drop.*
>
> **Distribution variant.** *I posted in r/VintageFashion with a piece ID question. 94 upvotes, 3 DMs. I posted on LinkedIn with a "lessons from sourcing in Florence" angle. 11 likes, 0 DMs. The community angle is 5x the signal of the broadcast angle. If you're Day 2 and getting crickets on LinkedIn, this is the move.*
>
> **Pick.** Distribution variant. Most concrete contrast, most useful to the cohort, fits Best Distribution Hustle category.
