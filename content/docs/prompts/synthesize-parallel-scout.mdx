## Why this works

You ran 3 scouts in parallel (Perplexity, Grok, Gemini) and you have 3 raw outputs sitting in 3 tabs. This prompt collapses them into one 500-word brief you upload to your Claude Project as `research-brief.md`. Forcing the synthesis to surface contradictions across the 3 sources is the whole point. Averaging the 3 outputs is what loses the signal. Disagreement is where weak claims expose themselves.

## Prompt

```txt
I ran the same niche scout query through 3 AI tools in parallel (Perplexity, Grok DeepSearch, Gemini). Below are the 3 raw outputs.

Synthesize them into a single brief I'll upload to my Claude Project as research-brief.md. Output exactly these 3 sections, nothing else:

1. COMPETITOR MAP. 5 to 8 entries, deduped across the 3 sources. For each: name, one-line positioning, who they target, one weakness, URL.

2. CONTRADICTIONS. Where do the 3 scouts disagree on facts, who's a real player, or what counts as a gap? Quote the disagreement directly. Don't smooth it over. If they all agree, say "no contradictions found" and move on.

3. GAPS I COULD WIN. The 1 to 2 gaps in this market I could actually win as a first-time builder shipping in 3 days. For each: one sentence on the gap, evidence from at least 2 of the 3 scouts that supports it.

Rules:
- Keep the entire brief under 500 words.
- No introduction, no closing, no sign-off. Plain markdown headings only.
- If a scout output is missing or empty, ignore that source and proceed with the others.

Here are the 3 scouts:

--- PERPLEXITY ---
[PASTE PERPLEXITY OUTPUT]

--- GROK ---
[PASTE GROK OUTPUT]

--- GEMINI ---
[PASTE GEMINI OUTPUT]
```

After Gemini outputs the brief, save the response as `research-brief.md` and upload it to your Claude Project knowledge (Files +). Every downstream prompt (the 4-box, landing-page spec, room scout) reads from this file.
