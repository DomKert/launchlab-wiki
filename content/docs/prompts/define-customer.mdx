## Why this works

Vague customer equals vague everything. This prompt forces you to pick one person and stop hedging. If your landing page dropped tomorrow, who's clicking the CTA first? That's who this sentence describes.

## Prompt

```txt
I'm working on [YOUR BUSINESS IDEA in 1 to 2 sentences].

Describe my customer in one sentence. Include age range, work or lifestyle context, and one behavior they already do that proves they'd buy this.

Write 3 variants. Each one specific enough that I could picture exactly one person. No "everyone," no "anyone who wants...". If you don't know enough to be specific, ask me 2 questions first.

After the 3 variants, rank them S / A / B tier and tell me which to use.
```

## Back Room sample answer

> S tier. **Women 35 to 59 in design, art, or architecture, who already buy European vintage once or twice a year and trust taste authority over deal-hunting.**
>
> A tier. Women 40 to 55 with creative jobs who shop Vestiaire occasionally but hate scrolling through 10,000 mediocre listings to find one good piece.
>
> B tier. Creative professional women who want fewer, better vintage pieces.
>
> Pick S. Names the age, the field, the existing behavior, and the decision-making filter.
