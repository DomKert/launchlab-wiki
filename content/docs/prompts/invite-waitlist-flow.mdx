## Why this works

Four touchpoints on the invite flow (waitlist page, confirmation, invite email, decline email) in one prompt. If you write them one at a time you'll drift out of voice. One shot keeps it consistent.

## Prompt

```txt
I'm building [PRODUCT]. It's invite-only. Write the copy for my full waitlist flow in 4 blocks:

Block 1. Waitlist hero (what they see before entering email)
- Headline (6 words or fewer)
- Subhead (one sentence on why invite-only matters here)
- Email field placeholder text
- Button text (2 to 4 words)

Block 2. Confirmation screen (right after they submit)
- Headline
- One sentence on what happens next
- One sentence on how to move up the list (share, refer, follow, whatever fits)

Block 3. Invite email (sent when they make the cut)
- Subject line (7 words or fewer)
- Opening line
- Middle (what the invite gets them, one paragraph, 2 to 3 sentences)
- CTA line and link framing

Block 4. Decline email (sent to people who don't make the cut, optional but write it)
- Subject line
- Two sentences, warm and clear

Voice. Exclusive without snobby. No "valued member," no "we regret to inform." Test. Would you reply to this email as a human, or flag it as marketing?
```

## Back Room sample answer (abbreviated)

> **Block 1 Waitlist hero.** Headline *By invite only.* Subhead *A private monthly release of European vintage. Curated, not hunted.* Placeholder *your email.* Button *Request an invite.*
>
> **Block 2 Confirmation.** Headline *You're on the list.* Next *We send invites on the 1st. Not everyone gets in. No hard feelings either way.* Move up *Forward this to one friend who actually gets it. We notice.*
>
> **Block 3 Invite email.** Subject *You're in for May.* Opening *Saturday, 9pm CET. 42 pieces.* Middle *You'll see the drop one hour before the general list. Everything's photographed straight, sized properly, priced EUR 90 to 280 except for 3 premium pieces. Sold is sold.* CTA *Preview the drop.*
>
> **Block 4 Decline email.** Subject *Not this round.* Body *We're keeping the list small to protect the quality of the drops. You're welcome to try again next month. No hard feelings either way.*
